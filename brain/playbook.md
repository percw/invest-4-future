# Playbook — Diamandis × Steinhardt 5-step framework

## Step 1: Hypothesis extraction

From the episode (or any source), identify 3-7 testable claims. Each must be:

1. **Falsifiable within 12-24 months.** "AI will transform everything" is
   not falsifiable. "TPU 8I captures >20 % inference share by 2028" is.
2. **Has an explicit mechanism.** Not "X is the future" — *how*, exactly.
3. **Has at least one observable catalyst.** Specific event that, if it
   happens, would update belief.

Reject vague themes.

## Step 2: Variant perception test

For each hypothesis, three forced questions:

1. What does sell-side / median analyst believe today?
2. Where does the speaker / source diverge from that?
3. Is the gap:
   - **(a) genuine non-consensus** with a real mechanism behind it?
   - **(b) already priced in** — consensus has updated, the divergence is stale?
   - **(c) hype** — divergence with no mechanism, just narrative?

Keep only **(a)**. Mark (b) and (c) in the hypothesis file with explicit reason.

## Step 3: Investability

For each surviving hypothesis, search for instruments meeting all mandate
filters (see `mandate.md`):

- Liquid public listing, ADV > $5M, mcap > $500M
- ≥ 50 % pure-play exposure to the thesis
- Not up > 100 % LTM without proportional EPS/revenue revision
- Prefer Oslo / EU listing for ASK eligibility when exposure is equivalent

If no single-stock fits, ETF is acceptable. If no ETF either, the thesis
is non-investable in public markets — note and skip.

## Step 4: Asymmetry

For each candidate, estimate over the stated horizon:

- **Upside %** if thesis confirms (state the driving assumption)
- **Downside %** if thesis falsifies (state the driving assumption)
- **Asymmetry = upside / |downside|** — must be ≥ 2.0

Be honest. A 30 % upside, 25 % downside is not a 2x asymmetric bet, no
matter how exciting the story.

## Step 5: Final selection

Output 3-5 recommendations max. If fewer than 3 qualify, output fewer.
**Never fill the quota to hit a number.** Empty weeks are a feature, not a bug.

## Cross-cutting principles

- **Mechanism > narrative.** Always.
- **Pre-commit to falsification.** Write down what would change your mind
  before you take the position.
- **Update beliefs on catalysts, not on prices.** Drawdown is not a signal.
- **Keep the rejected pile.** Half the alpha is in what you didn't buy.
