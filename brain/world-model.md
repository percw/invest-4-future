# World model — current macro/sector view

This is a living document. Updated weekly based on new episodes, data, or
falsifications. State the **assumptions** driving each view so they can be
challenged.

## Macro

- **Rate regime**: assumption — Fed on hold through 2026 H1, easing bias.
  Drives valuations on long-duration tech.
- **USD**: assumption — gradual weakening. Implies preference for
  non-USD-denominated equivalents where exposure is similar (ASK fit).

## AI / Compute

- **Bottleneck**: TSMC advanced packaging (CoWoS-L). All accelerator
  roadmaps converge here. Capacity 35k → 130k WPM end-2026, still trails
  demand.
- **Accelerator structure**: NVIDIA dominant in training; inference
  bifurcating — Google TPU 8I, custom hyperscaler silicon entering.
  Consensus assumption (NVIDIA holds 80 %+ forever) is challengeable.
- **HBM cycle**: HBM4 ramp pulls hybrid bonding equipment forward.
  BESI is sole-source.
- **Spending durability**: Anthropic revenue $1B → $9B → $30B in 16 mo
  signals enterprise AI capex compounding, not peaking.

## Robotics

- **Humanoid timing**: B2B pilots 2025-2026, commercial scale 2027-2028.
  Most pure-plays still private.
- **Industrial robotics (ABB, Fanuc) is a partial proxy**, not a pure
  exposure.

## Energy / Grid

- **AI power demand**: hyperscaler GW commits (Anthropic 5GW, etc.)
  re-rate utility / nuclear / grid names. Many already up significantly.

## Sectors I am not currently underwriting

- Consumer / retail (no thematic edge)
- Banks (mandate exclusion)
- Pure crypto (out of scope)
